# Real Estate Company FAQ

## Buying a Home

### What steps are involved in buying a home?
The typical steps include: getting pre-approved for a mortgage, finding a real estate agent, searching for properties, making an offer, home inspection, appraisal, closing. We guide you through each step.

### Do I need a real estate agent to buy a house?
You are not legally required to use an agent, but we strongly recommend it. Our agents provide expertise in negotiations, paperwork, and local market knowledge, often at no direct cost to buyers (seller typically pays commission).

### How long does the home buying process take?
From pre-approval to closing usually takes 30 to 60 days, depending on financing, inspections, and market conditions. We can give you a tailored timeline after your first consultation.

### What is a pre-approval and why do I need it?
A pre-approval is a lender’s conditional commitment to lend you a certain amount. It strengthens your offer and shows sellers you are a serious buyer. We can refer you to trusted lenders.

## Selling a Home

### How do you determine the value of my home?
We use comparative market analysis (CMA), recent sales of similar properties in your area, and current market trends. A free home valuation can be scheduled with one of our agents.

### What should I do to prepare my home for sale?
We recommend decluttering, deep cleaning, minor repairs, and staging. Our team can provide a free staging consultation and a checklist tailored to your property.

### How long will it take to sell my home?
It depends on price, location, and market. In a balanced market, the average is 30–60 days from listing to closing. We’ll set realistic expectations during our listing appointment.

### What fees or commissions should I expect when selling?
Commission structure is discussed at listing. Typically the seller pays a percentage of the sale price, which is often split between listing and buyer’s agent. We’ll outline all costs in a clear agreement.
